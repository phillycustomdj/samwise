Remembering Sam

Samuel Deuser Conterato

Samuel Deuser Conterato died unexpectedly at his home in Philadelphia, PA, on Monday, July 13th. His eclectic life began July 19th, 1980 in Marshfield, WI, to parents James Philip Conterato and Stephanie Lynn Deuser. As a young boy, Sam loved to play with Legos and watch Danger Mouse. He then turned his attention to Star Wars, MST3K, and Taekwondo, but you would also see him outside skiing and fishing. His high school years were marked by rather loose attendance at school, but complete dedication to his close-knit group of friends. Among other varied interests, he dove into the mosh pits at Warped Tour and competed in cross-country ski races at the American Birkebeiner.

After graduating from Marshfield Senior High, he studied computer science at UW-Eau Claire. It was during his time in Eau Claire that he was exposed to the glassblowing industry and was encouraged by his mentor to pursue a degree in this artistic science. While attending Salem Community College, he met his lifelong friends Jeremy Grant-Levine and Colin Maher.

After school, he moved to Philly and explored the city by longboard. Sam was initially involved in the glass industry, but then branched out into a variety of other work opportunities. Over the years, he was involved in neon sign creation and restoration, bartending, DJing, restaurant management, building management, and eventually went on to earn a degree in architectural design and CAD. More recently, he obtained extensive training in the art and science of brewing at Other Half Brewery from his good friend and mentor Bobby Bendily. His great love in life was his rescue animals, starting with his beloved pittie Tucker, and most recently his scrappy faux-Frenchie Frankie Milk Duds, who was Sam’s constant companion (and thorn in his side).

His interests in life ranged from books and media of every genre, collecting art (particularly pieces created by his many talented friends), skateboarding, Philly sports (but really just Gritty), video games, comedy shows, cooking, good food/beer/whiskey, and music (with a preference for bands with female lead singers). Throughout his life, he was a true autodidact, teaching himself whatever subject caught his interest; watching Jeopardy with him was a very humbling experience. He could out-gift Santa, gruffly handing over exactly the item you didn’t even know you needed.

Sam is survived by his immediate family—parents Jim and Lynn, his two sisters Terriss and Anna Conterato, his brother-in-law Benjamin Dunne, and his beloved grandmother Dorothy Deuser (who he dubbed “the queen of sewing”)—as well as the extended Deuserato family. He is also survived by his band of loyal Philly friends, as Sam understood and exemplified that a full life was full of friends. He was fiercely independent, ferociously loyal, and unwaveringly generous. He remains a presence in our hearts forever. The family would like to express our deepest gratitude to Colin and Jeremy for their compassion and presence during this difficult time. To all the people who considered Sam their friend, we find consolation knowing you will carry his spirit going forward.

In Sam’s Memory

Sam’s great love was his rescue animals. Donations in his memory can be sent to the Philadelphia Animal Welfare Society (PAWS) or your local animal shelter.
